DOOM (1993)

The original DOOM game, developed by id Software in 1993, is widely considered one of the most influential games of all time. The game pushed the envelope in terms of what was possible with immersive 3D graphics at the time, allowing players to explore the sci-fi corridors of Phobos, Deimos, and finally Hell itself while fighting enemies and collecting a variety of weapons. The gameplay was fast and frantic, with enemies coming from all around the player.

The game was also a pioneer in the areas of online networked play, with a competitive online mode that allowed players to battle against each other, as well as customized modifications to the game created by players. The game is credited with catapulting the FPS genre into the massive role it has in the games industry today.

id Software went on to continue the series with DOOM 2 and DOOM 3, as well as multiple spin-offs and other titles. id them went on hiatus from the series from 2004 until 2016, when they released the first new DOOM game since DOOM 3.
